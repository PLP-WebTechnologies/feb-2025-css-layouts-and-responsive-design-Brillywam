About Geology

Imagine the Earth as a giant storybook, with each rock, mountain, and valley telling a tale of fiery beginnings, relentless erosion, and dramatic transformations. Geology is the science that lets us read this story. From the molten magma deep within the Earth to the majestic mountains that touch the sky, geology helps us understand how our planet came to be.

Rocks of Kenya

Kenya is a treasure trove of geological diversity. Here, you’ll find:

Igneous Rocks

Born from fiery volcanic eruptions, these rocks, like basalt and granite, are found in regions such as the Great Rift Valley.

Sedimentary Rocks

Formed over millions of years from layers of sand and silt, these rocks are common in areas like the Turkana Basin.

Metamorphic Rocks

Transformed by heat and pressure, these rocks, such as marble and quartzite, can be found in regions like the Taita Hills.

Geological Wonders of Kenya

Kenya is home to some of the most breathtaking geological features in the world. Here are a few highlights:

The Great Rift Valley

A colossal scar in the Earth’s crust, stretching over 6,000 kilometers, the Rift Valley is a testament to the forces that shape our planet.

Mount Kenya

A dormant volcano and the second-highest mountain in Africa, Mount Kenya is a geological marvel and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Lake Turkana

Known as the "Jade Sea," this lake is the largest desert lake in the world and a hotspot for studying early human history.
